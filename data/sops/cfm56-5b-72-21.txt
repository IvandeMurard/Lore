STANDARD OPERATING PROCEDURE
SOP Reference: 72-21-00
Engine: CFM56-5B Series
Subject: N1 Vibration Monitoring and Escalation Procedures

1. SCOPE
This procedure covers the monitoring, assessment, and escalation of N1 shaft vibration readings on CFM56-5B engines installed on Airbus A320 family aircraft.

2. VIBRATION LIMITS

2.1 Normal Operating Range
- N1 vibration below 2.0 units: NORMAL — no action required.
- N1 vibration 2.0 to 4.0 units: MONITOR — log reading, compare to trend data, re-check within 2 flight cycles.

2.2 Escalation Thresholds
- N1 vibration 4.0 to 5.0 units: CAUTION — ground the engine for detailed inspection. Perform fan blade balance check per AMM 72-21-00-500.
- N1 vibration above 5.0 units: AOG — engine must not operate. Notify MCC immediately. Borescope inspection required per AMM 72-21-00-710.

3. MONITORING PROCEDURE

3.1 Record the following at each check:
- N1 vibration reading (units)
- N2 vibration reading (units)
- OAT (Outside Air Temperature) at time of reading
- Engine run time since last inspection
- Any unusual sounds or visual indicators

3.2 Trending
- Compare current reading to last 5 readings for the same engine/airframe combination.
- If reading has increased by more than 0.5 units from the previous check, flag for engineering review.
- If reading has been stable or decreasing, continue routine monitoring.

4. COLD WEATHER OPERATIONS

4.1 At OAT below 10°C, N1 vibration readings may show temporary increases of 0.5 to 1.0 units during the first 10 minutes of operation. This is within normal characteristics.
4.2 Allow engine to stabilize for minimum 10 minutes at idle before recording official vibration readings in cold conditions.
4.3 Do NOT escalate based on cold-start readings alone.

5. FAN SECTION INSPECTION

5.1 If vibration is above MONITOR threshold after temperature stabilization:
- Perform visual inspection of all fan blades for foreign object damage.
- Check fan blade tip clearances per AMM 72-21-01-200.
- Inspect fan containment case for rubbing indicators.
- Record findings and compare to last borescope report.

6. DOCUMENTATION
All vibration readings and assessments must be logged in the aircraft technical log and the engine trend monitoring system within 24 hours of the check.
